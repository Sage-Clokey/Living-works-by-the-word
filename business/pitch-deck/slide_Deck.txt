# Slide 1 — The Problem & Vision

## From the Age of Blocks to the Living Age

### The Problem

Modern civilization is built using **rigid mechanical systems**.

These systems:

* require massive energy to manufacture
* degrade and require replacement
* resist adaptation to changing environments
* separate human design from natural systems

Industries like construction, manufacturing, and infrastructure are still based on **industrial-age engineering**.

---

### The Opportunity

Living systems already solve many engineering problems.

Examples:

* trees self-assemble complex structures
* coral builds mineral architecture
* fungal networks distribute resources efficiently
* cells organize into tissues and organs

But our current tools only allow us to **analyze biology**, not **design with it**.

---

### Vision

> Enable humanity to **design with living systems instead of against them**.

Create a new design paradigm where structures, materials, and ecosystems are **grown rather than manufactured**.

This is the beginning of **the Living Age**.

---

# Slide 2 — The Solution

## Living Works: Biological Design Platform

**Living Works** is a platform for designing living systems using AI, bioinformatics, and morphogenesis modeling.

Instead of designing objects, it designs **growth processes**.

---

### Core Technologies

#### Morphogenesis Engine

3D simulation of biological growth.

Models:

* branching structures
* tissue growth
* environmental feedback
* biomechanical forces

---

#### AI Biological Design Interface

Natural language → biological design.

Example:

> “Design a branching structure optimized for wind resistance and water collection.”

The system generates

* growth models
* gene pathway suggestions
* structural simulations

---

#### BioCAD Platform

Computer-aided design tools for biology.

Capabilities:

* genome editing planning
* tissue architecture modeling
* ecosystem design
* living material simulation

---

### Example Applications

* Living architecture
* carbon-negative construction
* regenerative ecosystems
* biological manufacturing
* programmable materials
